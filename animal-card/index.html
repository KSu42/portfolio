Northern Cardinal

The northern cardinal is the state bird of more states than any other species.

  • Scientific name: Cardinalis cardinalis
  • Mass: 1.5 oz (Adult)
  • Length: 8.2 - 9.3 in.
  • Conservation status: Least Concern (Population stable)

With a body length of 21-23 cm, the northern cardinal is a mid-sized songbird with a distinctive crest on the head and a mask on the face. There are 19 subspecies which can be found from southern Canada all the way down through Mexico. A few cardinals lack the enzyme to convert ingested yellow pigments properly, making their beak and feathers yellow, but their face mask remains black.
